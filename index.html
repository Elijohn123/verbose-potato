Verbose

/vərˈboʊs/
Using more words than necessary; overly wordy or long-winded in speech or writing.
The professor was so verbose that a simple explanation took an entire hour.

Ubiquitous

/juːˈbɪkwɪtəs/
Present, appearing, or found everywhere.
Smartphones are ubiquitous in modern society.

Ephemeral

/ɪˈfɛmərəl/
Lasting for a very short time.
The beauty of a sunset is ephemeral but unforgettable.
